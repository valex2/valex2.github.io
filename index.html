Some Projects

Data-Driven Analysis of Glioblastoma Treatment Patterns

I analyzed unstructured medical records from patients with glioblastoma diagnoses to evaluate relevant, medication-based treatment patterns. I created novel line of therapy rules for regimen tracking and used time-to-next treatment estimators to determine which clinical approaches yield the most positive outcomes

Analysis of Population Activity in Balanced Spiking Neural Networks after Neuron Loss

I designed, developed, and tested a framework for simulating ischemic attacks within large populations of LIF neurons. I then built a novel toolbox of neuroscience-inspired functions that allows for the creation of robust, balanced spiking neural networks with excitatory and inhibitory cells.

I discovered that my simulated system is most robust to ablation near 15 - 30% network inhibition; potentially explaining in-vivo observations that excitatory:inhibitory neuron ratios fluctuate in this range.

Heart Abnormality Detection

I built a classification model for diagnosing heart abnormalities based on tomography features. I achieved a 93.58% classification accuracy with the regression-based classifier, a 25% improvement from the intitially implemented Bayesian approach

Laser Tag

Zane Chan and I designed a laser tag system on three RPis. We rapidly prototyped taggers, vests, and ambient light-canceling pads of photoresistors. We also developed our own communication protocols for 433 MHz radios, sound effects, and an active score tracking system

Ice Ice Baby: Mitigating Noise in Chirp Radar Sounding Systems

Patricia Strutz, Charlotte Nicks, Mauricio Rojas and I designed, prototyped, and implemented a signal processing system that maximizes the output signal to noise ratio (SNR) of linear frequency modulated ice penetrating radar data.

By optimizing our window functions, targeting specific noise bands, and cascading high-order filters we achieved a 41% increase in SNR and saw much improved ice-bed resolution

Raspberry Pi Computer System

I built a working computer system on a RPi from the ground up: starting in assembly and working from bare-metal. With no operating system safety net and extremely minimal use of libraries, nothing stood between me and the hardware; edit/compile/debug had to be done via cross-system tools (like GDB)

I developed the following from scratch: GPIO interface, memory allocator, string formatting and graphics libraries, shell, PS2 keyboard and mouse drivers. I also implemented an interrupt driven ring-buffer queue to optimize for keyboard efficiency, yielding a 75% decrease in bit loss

CaffE.Coli

Zane Chan, Melissa Nguyen and I designed and tested a novel biosynthetic pathway for the production of caffeine in E.coli. We custom-built a plasmid that co-opted endogenous guanine and devised our own system verification protocols

BCI for the Distracted Listener

Julian Cardenas and I are working on building a BCI for distracted listeners that manipulates playable media by monitoring EEG and eye-movements
